📐 SS1 Technical Drawing: Week 4
Care and Proper Use of Drawing Instruments

Learning Objectives: By the end of this lesson, students should be able to describe the care and maintenance of drawing instruments to ensure accuracy and longevity.

1. Overview

Proper care of drawing instruments ensures their longevity, accuracy, and the quality of work produced. Damaged or dirty instruments lead to inaccurate drawings, which can cause costly errors in engineering and construction.

2. Care of Specific Instruments

A. Compass

  • Keep the needle point sharp – A blunt needle slips and gives inaccurate centers
  • Store in a case to avoid bending the legs or joints
  • Do not force the joints – Adjust gently to avoid loosening
  • Replace leads regularly – Worn leads give thick, inaccurate lines
  • Keep joints clean – Dust in the joints causes stiffness

B. Pencils

  • Sharpen regularly to a fine conical point for accuracy
  • Use a sandpaper block to maintain the point without breaking lead
  • Use different grades for different line types – Hard pencils (2H, 4H) for construction lines, HB for lettering
  • Never press too hard – Causes grooves in paper and breaks lead

C. T-Square & Set Squares

  • Keep edges clean – Wipe with a damp cloth after use. Ink and graphite on edges stain drawings
  • Store flat to prevent warping. Never hang by the blade.
  • Never use as a cutting guide – Knife blades damage the working edges
  • Check edges for nicks regularly – Damaged edges give wavy lines
  • Avoid direct sunlight – Heat warps plastic and wood

D. Drawing Board

  • Keep the surface clean and smooth – Dirt causes paper to tear
  • Never cut on the drawing board – Use a separate cutting mat
  • Store vertically to prevent warping and dust collection
  • Check for flatness – A warped board gives inaccurate angles

E. Technical Pens

  • Clean after use to prevent ink clogging. Dried ink ruins the nib.
  • Store horizontally or cap tightly – Prevents ink from drying in the nib
  • Use the correct ink type – Only use drawing ink, not fountain pen ink
  • Don't shake – Causes air bubbles that break ink flow

F. French Curves & Templates

  • Clean ink/graphite after use with a soft cloth
  • Store flat to prevent bending or warping
  • Don't use as straight edges – Will damage the curves

3. General Rules of Care

  1. Always store instruments in a proper case or box – Prevents dust and damage
  2. Keep all instruments dry – Moisture causes rust on metal parts and warps wood
  3. Never drop instruments – Especially compasses and pens. Impact destroys accuracy.
  4. Clean instruments before and after use – Oils from hands attract dirt
  5. Handle with clean hands – Sweat and oil transfer to paper and cause smudges
  6. Use instruments only for their intended purpose – Don't use rulers as screwdrivers

4. Advantages of Proper Care

  • Instruments last longer – Saves money on replacements
  • Drawings are more accurate – Well-maintained tools don't introduce errors
  • Maintains professional quality – Clean, sharp tools give crisp lines
  • Saves time – No time wasted fixing or replacing broken tools

5. Disadvantages of Poor Care

  • Inaccurate drawings – Worn compasses draw wobbly circles
  • Damaged instruments – Bent T-squares can't draw straight lines
  • Increased costs – Frequent replacement is expensive
  • Poor-quality output – Dirty tools stain drawings and fail exams
  • Wastes time – Fighting with bad tools slows you down

2. Why should you never use a T-square as a cutting guide?
3. State 3 general rules for caring for drawing instruments.
