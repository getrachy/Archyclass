📐 SS1 Technical Drawing: Master Note
Circles - Basic Constructions

WAEC/NECO Focus: Circle constructions appear in 90% of Paper 1. You must show ALL construction lines. No construction lines = Zero marks even if circle is perfect.

1. Definition & Key Terms

  • Circle: Set of points equidistant from a fixed point called the centre
  • Radius (r): Distance from centre to any point on circle
  • Diameter (d): Line through centre touching circle at both ends. d = 2r
  • Chord: Any line joining 2 points on circle. Diameter is the longest chord
  • Tangent: Line that touches circle at exactly 1 point. Always 90° to radius at that point
  • Arc: Any part of the circumference
  • Segment: Area bounded by chord and arc
  • Sector: Area bounded by 2 radii and arc

2. Equipment Needed

  • Compass: Must have sharp needle and tight joint. Blunt needle slips
  • 2H Pencil: For construction lines - light and easy to erase
  • HB Pencil: For final outline
  • Ruler: For chords and tangents
  • Set squares: For 90° tangents

3. ALL BASIC CIRCLE CONSTRUCTIONS

A. Draw Circle of Given Radius (e.g., 30mm)

  1. Mark centre point O
  2. Set compass to 30mm using ruler
  3. Place needle at O, rotate smoothly 360°
  4. Draw centre lines: horizontal + vertical chain thin lines through O

B. Find Centre of Given Circle

  1. Draw any 2 chords AB and CD inside circle. Don't make them parallel
  2. Bisect chord AB using perpendicular bisector method
  3. Bisect chord CD using perpendicular bisector method
  4. Point where bisectors cross = Centre O
  5. Proof: Perpendicular bisector of any chord passes through centre
📺 YouTube Help: Search: "Find centre of circle geometry construction"

C. Draw Circle Through 3 Non-Collinear Points A, B, C

  1. Join AB and BC with light lines
  2. Construct perpendicular bisector of AB
  3. Construct perpendicular bisector of BC
  4. Bisectors intersect at O = centre of required circle
  5. Set compass: radius OA. Draw circle through A, B, C
  6. Exam Tip: If bisectors don't meet, your points are in straight line. Recheck.
📺 YouTube Help: Search: "Circle through 3 points construction"

D. Draw Tangent to Circle at Point T on Circle

  1. Given circle centre O, point T on circumference
  2. Join radius OT
  3. At T, construct 90° to OT using set square or compass
  4. That 90° line is the tangent
  5. Rule: Tangent is always perpendicular to radius at point of contact

E. Draw Tangents from External Point P to Circle

  1. Given circle centre O, point P outside circle
  2. Join OP
  3. Bisect OP. Midpoint = M
  4. With M as centre, radius MO, draw semicircle cutting given circle at T1 and T2
  5. Join PT1 and PT2. These are the 2 tangents
  6. Proof: Angle in semicircle = 90°. So ∠OT1P = 90°, making PT1 tangent
📺 YouTube Help: Search: "Construct tangent to circle from external point"

F. Inscribe Circle in Triangle (Incircle)

  1. Given triangle ABC
  2. Bisect angle A and angle B
  3. Bisectors meet at I = incentre
  4. From I, draw perpendicular to any side. This is radius r
  5. With I as centre, radius r, draw circle. Touches all 3 sides

G. Circumscribe Circle Around Triangle (Circumcircle)

  1. Given triangle ABC
  2. Bisect side AB - draw perpendicular bisector
  3. Bisect side BC - draw perpendicular bisector
  4. Bisectors meet at O = circumcentre
  5. With O as centre, radius OA, draw circle through A, B, C

5. Advantages of Learning Circle Constructions

  • Foundation for all curves – Ellipses, arcs, cycloids all need circles first
  • Used in all engineering fields – Gears, wheels, pipes, bolts
  • High WAEC marks – 10-15 marks per question
  • Develops accuracy – Teaches precision with compass

6. Disadvantages/Pitfalls Students Face

  • Slipping compass – Blunt needle or loose joint ruins accuracy
  • Heavy construction lines – Makes drawing dirty. Use 2H only
  • Erasing construction – WAEC gives zero if you erase. Leave them light
  • Wrong centre – If 3-point circle fails, recheck bisectors

7. WAEC/NECO Exam Tips

  1. Always draw centre lines – Horizontal + vertical chain thin through centre
  2. Label all points – O for centre, T for tangent point, P for external point
  3. Show all arcs – For bisection, show intersecting arcs clearly
  4. Use correct line types – Construction = thin continuous. Final circle = thick continuous
  5. Check with 2H before darkening – Ensure circle passes through all required points

8. Common Questions & Solutions

Q1: "Construct a circle of radius 25mm"
Ans: Method A. Don't forget centre lines. Q2: "Points A, B, C form triangle. Draw circle through them"
Ans: Method C. This is circumcircle. Q3: "From point P 60mm from centre O of circle radius 20mm, draw tangents"
Ans: Method E. You should get 2 tangents, each ≈56.6mm long.
Practice Assignment - Do ALL with compass only:
1. Draw circle radius 40mm. Find its centre using chord method to verify.
2. Mark 3 points: A(0,0), B(60,20), C(30,50). Draw circle through them.
3. Draw circle radius 25mm. Mark P 70mm from centre. Construct both tangents from P.
4. Draw triangle sides 70mm, 60mm, 50mm. Inscribe a circle.
5. Draw triangle sides 80mm, 70mm, 60mm. Circumscribe a circle.
