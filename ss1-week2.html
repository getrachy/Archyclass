3. Drawing Instruments and Materials

Overview

These are the tools and materials used to produce accurate and neat technical drawings. WAEC Paper 1 always tests your knowledge of these instruments, their uses, and how to care for them.

Note: You must be able to identify each instrument by sight and state its use in exams.

Types of Drawing Instruments

A. Measuring & Drawing Tools

T-Square – Used to draw horizontal lines and as a guide for set squares. Never use as a cutting guide. Keep edge clean.

Set Squares (45° and 30°/60°) – Used to draw angles and vertical lines. Store flat to prevent warping.

Ruler/Scale Rule – For measuring and drawing straight lines. Not for drawing lines - measuring only.

Protractor – For measuring and drawing angles from 0° to 180°. Keep clean for visibility.

Compass – For drawing circles and arcs. Keep needle point sharp. Store in case to avoid bending.

Divider – For transferring measurements and dividing lines equally. Both legs have needle points.

French Curves – For drawing irregular curves.

Flexible Curve – Bendable tool for custom curves.

B. Drawing Surfaces

Drawing Board – Flat surface to place drawing sheets. Standard WAEC size: 500mm x 350mm. Keep clean, store flat.

Drawing Table – Adjustable surface for professional use.

C. Writing/Marking Instruments

Pencils (H, HB, B grades) – Different hardness for different line weights. Keep sharp with conical point.

Technical Pens/Rapidograph – For ink drawings with consistent line width. Clean after use.

Eraser – For corrections. Use soft eraser only. Keep clean to avoid smudges.

Erasing Shield – Protects surrounding lines when erasing.

D. Drawing Materials

Drawing Paper/Cartridge Paper – Standard paper for pencil drawings. WAEC uses A3 size (420mm x 297mm).

Tracing Paper – Transparent paper for copying drawings.

Drawing Film/Vellum – Durable, professional drafting material.

Drawing Pins/Clips – For securing paper to the board.

Masking Tape – Holds paper without damaging it. Never use cello tape.

Advantages of Good Instruments

  • Produce accurate, clean drawings
  • Increase speed and efficiency
  • Improve professional quality

Disadvantages

  • Quality instruments can be expensive
  • Require maintenance and care
  • Some instruments are fragile
