📐 SS1 Technical Drawing: Week 8
Construction of Basic Shapes

Learning Objectives: By the end of this lesson, students should be able to construct lines, angles, triangles, and circles using compasses and rulers only - no protractor.

1. Overview

Geometric construction is the process of drawing shapes using only a compass, straightedge (ruler), and pencil - no protractor, no measurements.

These constructions form the foundation of all technical drawing. Every complex shape is built from these basic constructions.

Key Rule: All construction lines must be light (2H pencil) and should NOT be erased. They prove you used correct methods.

2. Basic Constructions

A. To Bisect a Line AB

Steps:
  1. Draw line AB of any length
  2. With A as centre, radius > ½AB, draw arcs above and below AB
  3. With B as centre, same radius, draw arcs to intersect the first arcs at C and D
  4. Join CD. CD bisects AB at 90° at point M
  5. AM = MB and ∠AMC = 90°

B. To Bisect an Angle ABC

Steps:
  1. With B as centre, any radius, draw an arc cutting BA at P and BC at Q
  2. With P as centre, radius > ½PQ, draw an arc
  3. With Q as centre, same radius, draw an arc to intersect at R
  4. Join BR. BR bisects ∠ABC. ∠ABR = ∠RBC

C. To Construct 60° Angle

Steps:
  1. Draw line AB
  2. With A as centre, any radius, draw an arc cutting AB at P
  3. With P as centre, same radius, cut the arc at Q
  4. Join AQ. ∠BAQ = 60°

D. To Construct 90° Angle

Steps:
  1. Construct 60° as above to get point Q
  2. With Q as centre, same radius, cut the arc at R
  3. Bisect ∠QAR to get 30°
  4. 60° + 30° = 90°. Or simply erect a perpendicular using line bisection method

E. To Construct an Equilateral Triangle

Steps:
  1. Draw base AB
  2. With A as centre, radius AB, draw an arc
  3. With B as centre, same radius AB, draw an arc to intersect at C
  4. Join AC and BC. ABC is equilateral: all sides equal, all angles 60°

F. To Construct a Square

Steps:
  1. Draw base AB
  2. At A, erect a perpendicular AD = AB using 90° construction
  3. With D as centre, radius AB, draw an arc
  4. With B as centre, same radius AB, draw an arc to intersect at C
  5. Join BC and DC. ABCD is a square

G. To Draw a Circle Through 3 Points A, B, C

Steps:
  1. Join AB and BC
  2. Bisect AB and BC using perpendicular bisectors
  3. The bisectors meet at O - the centre of the circle
  4. With O as centre, radius OA (or OB or OC), draw the circle
