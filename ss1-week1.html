📐 SS1 Technical Drawing: Week 1
Introduction to Technical Drawing

Learning Objectives: By the end of this lesson, students should be able to define technical drawing, list its types, and state its advantages and disadvantages.

1. What is Technical Drawing?

Technical drawing, also called drafting or engineering drawing, is a form of visual communication used to clearly and accurately represent objects, structures, or systems.

It uses standardized symbols, lines, and conventions to convey information that can be understood universally by engineers, architects, designers, and technicians anywhere in the world.

Unlike artistic drawing, technical drawing is not about aesthetics. It is about accuracy, clarity, and precision so that anyone reading the drawing can manufacture or build the object exactly as intended.

2. Types of Technical Drawing

OrthographicShows an object from multiple 2D views - front, top, sideEngineering components

3. Advantages of Technical Drawing

  1. Universal Language – Understood by professionals worldwide regardless of spoken language
  2. Precision – Provides exact measurements and specifications
  3. Error Detection – Helps identify design flaws before construction/manufacturing
  4. Legal Document – Serves as a contract between client, designer, and builder
  5. Time & Cost Saving – Reduces guesswork and rework during production
  6. Permanent Record – Can be stored and referenced years later
  7. Quality Control – Used to inspect finished products against design

4. Disadvantages of Technical Drawing

  1. Requires Training – Cannot be read or produced without specialized knowledge
  2. Time-Consuming – Manual drafting takes significant time
  3. Costly Errors – Small mistakes can lead to expensive manufacturing errors
  4. Damage Risk – Paper drawings can be torn, lost, or damaged
  5. Misinterpretation – Poor drafting can confuse builders/engineers
  6. Updates Difficult – Changing manual drawings requires redrawing

5. Why Study Technical Drawing?

  • Foundation for all engineering and architectural careers
  • Develops spatial reasoning and visualization skills
  • Required for WAEC, NECO, and JAMB technical subjects
  • Useful in fields: Mechanical, Civil, Electrical, Architecture, Surveying, Fashion Design
