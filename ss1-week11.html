📐 SS1 Technical Drawing: Week 11
Freehand Sketching & Pictorial Drawing

Learning Objectives: By the end of this lesson, students should be able to create freehand sketches and differentiate between isometric, oblique, and perspective drawings.

1. Overview

Freehand sketching is drawing without instruments like rulers or compasses. It is used for quick idea capture, design development, and on-site notes.

Pictorial drawing shows an object as it appears to the eye - in 3D form. Unlike orthographic projection, pictorial drawings show height, width, and depth in one view.

2. Freehand Sketching Techniques

A. Materials

  • Soft pencil (HB or B) – For smooth, dark lines
  • Plain paper – No grid lines to rely on
  • Eraser – For corrections
  • No ruler, no compass – All lines drawn freehand

B. Rules for Good Freehand Sketching

  1. Hold pencil correctly – 30mm from tip, relaxed grip
  2. Draw from shoulder/elbow, not wrist – For smooth, long lines
  3. Use light construction lines first – Box in the object, then add details
  4. Work from large to small – Overall shape before details
  5. Keep lines firm and confident – Avoid hairy, scratchy lines
  6. Practice basic strokes – Horizontal, vertical, 45°, circles
  7. Use proportion, not measurement – Estimate ratios by eye

3. Types of Pictorial Drawing

A. Isometric Drawing

  • Angles: 30° to horizontal on both sides. Vertical lines remain vertical.
  • Scale: All dimensions are true length. No foreshortening.
  • Appearance: Most realistic of technical pictorials. Shows 3 faces.
  • Use: Engineering catalogs, assembly instructions, WAEC/NECO
  • Advantage: Easy to draw and measure from
  • Disadvantage: Circles appear as ellipses - hard to draw

B. Oblique Drawing

  • Angles: Front face is true shape. Receding lines at 45° (Cavalier) or 30°/60°
  • Scale: Cavalier - full depth. Cabinet - half depth (more realistic)
  • Appearance: Front view undistorted. Depth looks exaggerated.
  • Use: When front face has circles or complex detail
  • Advantage: Circles on front face stay as circles - easy to draw
  • Disadvantage: Looks distorted if depth is long

C. Perspective Drawing

  • Angles: Lines converge to vanishing points on horizon
  • Types: 1-point, 2-point, 3-point perspective
  • Appearance: Most realistic. Objects get smaller with distance.
  • Use: Architectural presentations, artistic rendering
  • Advantage: Looks like human eye sees it
  • Disadvantage: Cannot measure dimensions. Complex to construct

5. Advantages of Pictorial Drawings

  • Easy to understand – Non-technical people can visualize the object
  • Shows 3D form in one view – No need to imagine from multiple views
  • Good for presentations – Clients prefer pictorials
  • Freehand sketches are fast – Capture ideas quickly

6. Disadvantages

  • Cannot show true dimensions – Except isometric front faces
  • Distorted appearance – Especially oblique and perspective
  • Not suitable for manufacturing – Workshops need orthographic
  • Freehand requires skill – Bad sketches confuse more than help

7. Steps to Create Isometric Sketch

  1. Draw light isometric axes – Vertical + 30° left + 30° right
  2. Block in overall size – Draw enclosing box using true dimensions
  3. Add major features – Cutouts, holes, steps
  4. Add details – Fillets, small holes
  5. Darken final lines – Use thick lines for visible edges
  6. Erase construction lines – Leave sketch clean
Practical Assignment:
1. Freehand sketch a matchbox in isometric projection.
2. State 3 differences between isometric and oblique drawing.
3. Why are circles difficult to draw in isometric?
4. Draw a cube of 40mm sides in: a) Isometric b) Cabinet Oblique
5. List 4 rules for good freehand sketching.
